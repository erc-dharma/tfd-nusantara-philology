    Description of the witness

    • Manuscript Identity:
      • Settlement: Leiden
      • Repository: Leiden Univeristy Library
      • Identifier: Cod.Or.3429
    • Physical Description:
      • Support: Tree-bark
      • Extent: 27 folios , 27cm x 24cm
    • Layout Description: The texts run from folio A1 to A27, and from B1 to B26, B27 is left blank. The beginning of a text is precedeed by the bindu na godang B1 whereas the division into smaller sections of the texts is stresssed by the bindu na metmet B2. From from B1 to B9 the internal division of the text are stressed by another type of bindu na metmet in which its upper right-side has been elongated shaping a single black line BL. The black line preceedes and runs parallel to the first line of the text, until the right-side margin. On these same folios, the each small section of text is accompanied by a drawing, rapresenting different antrophomorphic figures.

    Translation

    1.

    Introduction

    1

    Teaching/Instruction of the core of the mountain-oracle to be observed either during the ceremony horja Sita-sita, or people make us into datu during a great war, or people make us into datu in inaugurating a new settlement, or we are celebrating a marriage, or we are offering unhusked rice on the rice field for the feast-giving group, or we are bringing our child to the river, or during the porsahan ceremony of the man who died old, or during the magic warfare.

    2.

    Invocations and enumeration of the datus through which the instructions have been handed down

    1

    So, observe the way in which our buffalo falls, in order that it will be known if we win the dispute, it will be known if we succeed, it will be known if we fail, from the words of our mountain-oracle here, said our Guru, Guru Agan ni aji oh!

    2

    So, let me teach it to you, O Guru Tumurun (descent) ni aji; so let it come to you, Guru Si Dupangon (helix-shaped leaves) ni aji, great wandering Datu who comes to the world of men, who takes care of the gold, the yellow one. Let it come to you, Oh Grandfather of Mangaleban of the marga Haro from the Sirait land; let it come to you Datu Panggual (musician) ni aji oh!

    3

    Next, as for the marompuni name of Ompu Tuvan Binur hata ni aji, a great wandering Datu [of] every part of the whole worlds, who takes care of the gold, the yellow one, [whose] hands are white-spotted; [whose] fingers are rurus (dropping? slender?); since the coins are in the great lobuan, I am the invisible bride-price, from the area of Palipi, of the marga Sinaga, Grandfather Ratus (Hundred) who has never lost any magic war relying on the great Pangulubalang Duva Raja Ojim, relying on the great (magical preparation) Panuruni Balagori, relying on (the aggressive magic of) the Pabuhu Tanduk Si anggasana Si manoktohi; who cannot be sent back home by the male, [for instance] a datu; who cannot be sent away by the woman [for instance] a great Si Baso.
