Description of the witness

  • Manuscript Identity:
    • Settlement: Leiden
    • Repository: Leiden Univeristy Library
    • Identifier: Cod.Or.3429
  • Physical Description:
    • Support: Tree-bark
    • Extent: 27 folios , 27cm x 24cm
  • Layout Description: The texts run from folio A1 to A27, and from B1 to B26, B27 is left blank. The beginning of a text is precedeed by the bindu na godang B1 whereas the division into smaller sections of the texts is stresssed by the bindu na metmet B2. From from B1 to B9 the internal division of the text are stressed by another type of bindu na metmet in which its upper right-side has been elongated shaping a single black line BL. The black line preceedes and runs parallel to the first line of the text, until the right-side margin. On these same folios, the each small section of text is accompanied by a drawing, rapresenting different antrophomorphic figures.

This segment is a significant example of the slightly different functions played by the conjunction asa. In the first occurrence is used for introducing a sequel of actions, and more specifically, for introducing the main clause of this sequence. In the second place, it introduces the consequence that the main clause has for the secondary clause. In this last occurrence, asa is identical to the other conjunction umbaen. In the text, it will not be always so clear which of these functions should be attributed to asa, and for this reason I will consider it generally as a conjunction introducing a sequence of actions, which also in TBG 317 is defined as its original function.
