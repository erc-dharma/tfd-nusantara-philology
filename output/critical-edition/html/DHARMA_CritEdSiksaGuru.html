1.

the pure circle of three bodily domains

1.1

A:1vB:1vndah nihan(1) varahakna mami rumuhun ri saṅ sevaka dharma, katəkan kita ruməṅəkna kavivekan(2) saṅ hyaṅ dharma, mvaṅ kabyavasthānikaṅ(3) saṅ hyaṅ ajñāna, hana trikāya maṇḍala(4) pariśuddha, ṅa(5), inujarakən saṅ paṇḍita ri saṅ sevaka dharma, nyāpan tahan kveki(6) ulah śabda lavan ambək, yan kva(7) hana malīna(8) ri hr̥dayanta(9), ya teka prih(10) pəgəṅən, rakṣa pahayu juga ya, apan hetuniṅ(11) manəmvakən(12) sukhaduhkha halahayu.

Translation

As follows I will teach first to the servant of the law. You will succeed in listening to the discussion of the holy law and the determined state of the holy gnosis. There is what is called the pure circle of three bodily domains, uttered by the teacher to the servant of law. That is probably the action, speech, and mind. If so, there is a feeling of loss in your heart, that is the practice that should be restrained. You should just guard them and fix them, for it is the cause of finding joy and sorrow, and good and bad.

1.2

yan pinahala ulahta, śabdanta, ambəkta(13), niyata kapaṅguhnikaṅ(14) hala denta yan maṅkana(15), yan pinahayu ulahta, A:2r śabdanta(16), ambəkta, avas kapaṅguhnikaṅ hayunta(17) yan maṅkana, ya ta(18) mataṅyan hana paribhāṣa kumvaṅ madyus-dyus(19) vvay tinəmunya kasvabhāvaniṅ(20) janma, hala tuməmu halanya, hayu tuməmu hayunya, maṅkana lvirniṅ(21) janma.

Translation

If your action, your speech and your mind are unrighteous, in that case you surely obtain the wrong. If your action, your speech, your heart are righteous, in that case it is clear that you obtain the virtue. That is why there is a proverb “Thus one repeatedly baths, the water is touched by the original nature of the human: bad meets its bad, good meets its good”. Likewise is human nature.

1.3

ndya ḍr̥ṣṭānta, iriki dadyanya, hala deniṅ halanya, hayu deniṅ hayunya, nihan upadyanta, hana ya maṇik anargha ṅaranya, tan kinavruhan(22) mulyanya, tan paprameya ri(23) rahayu, kaliṅanya ikaṅ maṇik anargha, lavan maṇik sarveṣṭa(24), yvagya karikāpusənta ri gaṅsa, tambaga lavan timah, aparan(25) ta yvagyanika, niyatār A:2v mijil śrīnika lavan dhanuhnika, maṅka pva yvagya(26) paṅapusanta irikaṅ maṇik anargha, paṅikətanta irikaṅ maṇik sarveṣṭa(27).

Translation

What are the illustrations with regard to the consequence of this, [namely]: bad from its bad, good from its good? As follows that will be used as the special condition by you: there are what are called priceless jewels, its value can not be known, incomparable in terms of beauty. The meaning is: the priceless jewel and the most coveted jewel. It is fitting to be bound to bell-metal, copper and tin. What is the appropriateness? It is evident that its splendour and its shine come out, if your binding for the priceless jewel and your tieing for the most coveted gem are appropriate according to that manner.

1.4

nihan liṅnira(28) ri saṅ hyaṅ śāstra lpas ca vuṁkunuṁ(29) ceva, aḍika udika tatte tatur tathya tuṅgəṅ, ta deva nigaḍyate, apan(30) sinaṅguh rambut ləpas, ṅa, vulu pasagi, ṅa, hana uḍika, hana tater, hana sorasa, ṅa, ika ta kabaih yvagya karika paṅapusan ta irikaṅ maṇik anargha, paṅikətan ta irikaṅ maṇik sarveṣṭa(31), taham pih yāpusən ta ri gaṅsa tambaga lavan timah, aparan ta yvagyanika, paṅapusan ta irikaṅ maṇik anargha, paṅikəA:3rtan ta irikaṅ maṇik sarveṣṭa, nahan liṅnya(32) ri saṅ hyaṅ śāstra, ləpas ca vuṁkunuṁ ceva(33), Aḍi Ikaṁ, Uḍi Ika:, tate:r tatur tathya tuṅgəṅ nu yvagyanika

Translation

As follows is the word of the holy treatise: ‘lpas ca vuṅkunuṅ ceva, aḍika udika tatte tatur tathya tuṅgəṁ, ta deva nigaḍyate’. Because what is considered the loose hair is the square hair; there is the uḍika, the tater, and the lice. All of them are appropriate to be bound to the priceless jewel and chained to the most coveted jewel. No, it is not like that, they should be bound to the bell-metal, copper and tin. Why is that appropriate? The binding to the priceless jewel, the chaining to the most coveted jewel, such is the word of the holy treatise ‘lpəs ca vuṅkunuṅ cova, aḍi ikaṅ, uḍi ikā, tater tatur tatya tuṅgəṅ nu yvagyanika’.

1.5

hana ya mas kanaka santaṅva(34), ṅa, mas kanaka jātirūpa, sakati mulyanya, vrəttya(35) inapisan pituṅ apitu, matəmahan ta ya satahil i vatu(36) bratnya(37), ndya ya teka tuṅgəṅ, tan limbak(38) tan ləgə(39), an inapi muvah, apan samaṅkana juga, pinakadadiniṅ(40) mas kabaih, ndah ya teka yvagya rahayu paṅapusanta irikaṅ maṇik anargha, paṅikətanta irikaṅ maṇik sarveṣṭa(41).

Translation

There is what is called the glowing gold, beautiful gold, its value is one kati. The course of action will be [as follows]: it is layered seven times seven, transformed into weighing one tahil of stone. How can it be strong, not undulating, unwavering, when it is set ablaze again? For only in this way is the outcome of all the gold, and then the binding of priceless jewels and the tying of the most coveted jewel are appropriate and good.

1.6

ya ta kāpusan deni gaṅsa(42) taA:3vmbaga lavan timahB:5r, tar mijil śrīnika lavan dhanuhnika, aparan(43) tar yuktinika(44), salah nija, salah karma, salah svabhava(45) ṅaranika, iva maṅkana ta saṅ hyaṅ dharma viśeṣa, sira maṇik anargha, ṅuniveh avakta, yeka maṇik sarveṣṭa ṅaranya.

Translation

It is [i.e. gold] bound with bell-metal, copper and tin, its splendour and its shine do not come out. What are the reasons they are not appropriate? Wrong inner nature, wrong action, wrong nature. Just so is the holy supreme dharma, it is a priceless gem; and also your body: that is the most coveted gem. Then what is considered bell-metal, copper, and tin, are not appropriate to be bound to the priceless jewel, [and as] the tying of the most coveted jewel.

1.7

ndah ta yāṅkən gaṅsa tambaga lavan timah, ika tan yvagya rakətakna(46) iriB:5vkaṅ maṇik anargha, paṅikətanta irikaṅ maṇik sarveṣṭa.

Translation

As follows are the bad actions, improper speech, dirty heart towards all humans, especially toward the learned teacher. The meaning is: bad actions, improper speech, and a dirty heart are not appropriate; the holy dharma shall be reflected upon. Furthermore, your body should be protected, especially for the learned teacher. No! Love is not proper, the repetition of your human condition, one ceases to love having children, having a wife, building a family, having relatives and a mother in law. He is unable to protect [himself] from bad actions, improper speech, a dirty heart towards all humans, and especially towards ascetics. How would your protection of your body be appropriate? That is the glowing santaṅva as said by the master. What is called a glowing gold is the good action, the good speech, the good heart. Those should be protected by the holy dharma.

1.8

nihan ya, ulah mahala, śabda tar yukti, ambək(47) magələh ri kapva kaṅ(48) janma, ṅuniveh ri saṅ paṇḍita, kaliṅanya tan yvagya kari ulah mahala, śabda tar yukti, ambək magələh, rasāA:4rkən i saṅ hyaṅ dharma, lavan arakṣa(49) śarīranta, ṅuniB:6rveh ri saṅ paṇḍita, taham pih(50) tar yukti ika, punaruktanikaṅ(51) kavvaṅanta, hilaṅ masihniṅ manak(52) maṅrabi, manak(53) makadaṅ mvaṅ renanta(54), ya tan vnaṅ rumakṣa ikaṅ ulah mahala śabda tar yukti ambək magələh ri kapva kaṅ janma, ṅuniveh ri saṅ sādhu, ndya(55) yvagya paṅrakṣanta, ri śarīranta, yeka(56) santaṅva liṅ saṅ paṇḍita, kanaka santaṅva ṅaranyaB:6v, ulah rahayu, śabda rahayu, ambək rahayu, ya rakṣakna(57) saṅ hyaṅ darma.

Translation

This is the reason why it is called the pure circle of three bodily domains, as said by the master: tri means three. The circle of three bodily domains is pure. So it is said. Thus are the three bodily domains, as said by the master.

1.9

nihan mataṅyan trikāya maṇḍala pariśuddha ṅaranya, liṅ saṅ paṇḍita, tri ṅaraniṅ təlu, śuddha ta ya trikāya(58) maṇḍaləm, ityuktam(59), nahan(60) trikāya(61) liṅ saṅ paṇḍita.

Translation

As follows is the beginning of the love of a learned teacher toward the servant of the dharma. It is pure in the good thought. Do it quickly! Do not be obsessed with dirty thoughts. The heart and mind are pure. Then, purify the mind and the set of your emotions! You should be aware when thinking; you should practice the activity of begging for alms (bhikṣālakṣanam) with full attention.

1.10

nihan(62) *təmbəynya sih saṅ paṇḍita ri saṅ sevaka dharma, pariśuddha ta ri ambək(63) rahayu, usən(64) hayva kasəlapan ambək magələh, nirmalaṁ hr̥dayam(65) citam, ndah pahaliṅlaṅ kaṅ(66) manah pvāmbək(67) tiṅət maṅaṅən-aṅən, prihən bhikṣālakṣanam(68).

Translation

Here are your characteristics, O holy man. Do not be attached to the servants. It is evident that the hair has been cut by it. Cut it [so that] there is no obstruction or hindrance. That is purity, you should cut it before and you should control it. It is what is called … made flower offering with water. In this manner you serve the learned teacher. Do not commit the sin of violating the guru’s bed, [which is] a misfortune for the world; [the pupil] should fear him. You should be devoted and submit to the teacher, you should carry out [acts of] extreme respect toward the teacher, including to the learned masters. You should arrive to its goal.

1.11

nihan lakṣaṇanta viku(69), hayva tr̥ṣṇa ri hulun juga, byakta taṅ raC:5rmbut pinəgatan, ya tan(70) pəgati, tan kavighāta(71) malaṅ kva hana, ya tika pariśuddha timpalakna(72) kunaṅ(73) rətakna, ya ta mas r̥kr̥t(74) masəkar vañva, ṅa, maṅkana ta kita masevaA:5rka *di(75) saṅ paṇḍita, hayva ta kita gumavayakna gurutalpaka(76), jagat upadrava, katakuta(77) ri sira, bhaktyānukul(78) ta kita ri saṅ guru, gavayakna kasupraṇata(79), di(80) saṅ guru, təka ri saṅ paṇḍita, dataṅ ta ri kasādhyanya.

Translation

In this manner you are the servant of the learned master, even more so of the king. Do not be unfaithful toward the king. [You] will be punished, hated, you will be afraid of him, so that you will be sold and horribly die. You should be loyal to the king, so that [you] will be affected by its result. Thus you, the elder (rāma), the kalaṅ, the gusti, the chief of the village (patih banva), and also the quality (dharma) of a balanced mind in people like you. You should not have a biased voice in the legal proceeding, do not lean left and right with respect to good and bad qualities. It is not appropriate [as the] behaviour of experienced men, says the holy scriptures. That should not be followed by you. That is the reason why [if you follow them] the three domains of body and mind (trikāya maṇḍalam cittam) are not achieved. Then the servants of the dharma should devote their full attention to what is called the sacred circle of the three pure bodily domains. It is the beginning of love of the learned teacher towards the servants of the dharma.

maṅkana ta kita hulun ri saṅ paṇḍita, ṅuniveh ri saṅ prabhu, tar gavayakna prabhu drohaka(81), dəṇḍana vinigraha(82) katakuta(83) ri sira, marapvan ta kita na kajval(84) pati biṣama, bhaktya(85) ta kita ri saṅ(86) prabhu, mataṅyan təka ri kasādhyanya(87), maṅkana(88) ta kita rāma, kalaṅ(89), gusti, patih ri banva, kunaṅ dharma tulājñānaniṅ kadi kita, hayva ta kita tiA:5vmpaṅ svara ri byavahāra, tan paṅivakən təṅən(90) ri guṇadosa, tar yukti purihniṅ vvaṅ atuha, ujar saṅ hyaṅ āgama, hayva tutaknanta, ya ta mataṅyan tar katəkan trikāyamaṇḍalam cittam(91), nihan saṅ hyaṅ trikāya maṇḍala pariśuddha, ṅa, kayatnakna saṅ sevaka dharma, ya təmbəynya sih saṅ paṇḍita, ri saṅ sevaka dharma.

2.

Second teacher’s love:daśaśīla

2.1

ika hana ta(92) kapindva(93) asih(94) saṅ paṇḍita ri saṅ sevaka dharma, ya daśaśīla, ṅa, ndya ya nihan, śrotra(95), cakṣuh, ghrāṇa, jihvā, vāk, tvak, pāṇi, pāyūpastha(96), pāda, *ini(97) rəṅə̄kna saṅ sevaka dharma.

Translation

There is a second love of the learned teacher towards the servant of the dharma, which is called the ten rules (daśaśīla); they are the following: ears, eyes, nose, tongue, mouth, skin, hands, genitals, anus, legs. These are what the servant of the dharma should hear.

2.2

śrotra(98) ṅaraniṅ taliṅa, apa viṣayaniṅ taliṅa A:6r, pinakadvāraniṅ(99) maṅrəṅə̄ ikaṅ śabda hala hayu, maṅrəṅə̄(100) kita śabda manis arum(101) enak manahta, maṅrəṅə̄(102) pva kita śabda mahala, sākṣāt(103) tan kahiḍəp(104) doṣanika, tan piniṇḍvakən(105) kārya(106) hayu ri saṅ guru, ya ta kayatnakna(107) saṅ sevaka dharma, tuli maṅrəṅə̄ aji saṅ(108) viku.

Translation

Śrotra means ears. What is the sphere of the ears? They serve as a gate to hear bad and good words. When you hear sweet and fragrant words, your heart becomes comfortable. When you hear a bad word, oppression is what is experienced, that is its fault. Do not treat good duty towards the teacher as a second priority. That is what the servants of the dharma should pay attention to, who are deaf in hearing the scriptures of the holy man.

2.3

cakṣuh ṅaraniṅ mata, apa visayaniṅ mata, anon ta ya(109) hala hayu, aveh sukhaduhkha, yar apa(110) ika(111) aveh sukha(112), pinakapanonta ri ulah rahayu laṅsar kita denya, yan apa ika aveh(113) duhkha, mulata mas ri paḍanya janma len saka rika, lumaku(114) ta kita mareṅ sabhān(115), anon(116) maA:6vra kita vvaṅ(117) cheda, vuḍug, tugəl, təlihən(118), *buḍaya(119), tikəl, kəjəṅ, *kəkəṅ(120), salvirniṅ virūpa(121) haneṅ(122) rat, ṅuniveh buta tulīniṅ panvan paṅrəṅə̄(123), hayva peda(124) kunaṅ yan kita(125) anvan vvaṅ *chedakṣara(126), hayva ta kita maṅkana, *caṇḍaparihāra(127) ṅaranya, apan tar kinavruhan ikaṅ *nijakarma(128), ṅuniveh vvaṅ duhkha denika(129), ya ta kayatnakna saṅ sevaka dharma.

Translation

Cakṣuh means eyes. What is the sphere of the eyes? To see good and bad, that causes joy and sorrow. How can they cause the joy? They serve as your instrument to see the good deeds, [that] come down from there. How can that bring sorrow? Because of that you should see ‘the gold’ from other fellow human beings. You walk to the public place, you see people with disabilities, leprosy, invalidity, goitre, starvation (?, buḍaya), deformities, epilepsy, rheumatism, all kinds of abnormalities that exist in humans, let alone those who are blind and deaf of the eyes and ears. Do not hurt somebody who has a disabled bodies. You should not be like that. It is called avoiding strong emotion (caṇḍaparihāra), for the innate reaction is not seen, let alone people hurt because of it. That is what the servant of the dharma should pay attention to.

2.4

ghrāṇa ṅaraniṅ iruṅ, apa viṣayaniṅ iruṅ, pinakapaṅambuṅ ta gandha surabhi, asurabhi, *asādhāraṇa(130), surabhi(131) ṅaranya vaṅi, asurabhi ṅaranya(132) abva lavan hapək, asādhāraṇa ṅaranya *haṅit, mataṅyan tri(133) pabhedaniṅ gaA:7rndha, aṅambuṅ pva(134) kita gandha vaṅi, enak manahta, aṅambuṅ pva(135) kita abva(136) hapək, tar enak manahta, ya mətu(137) krodhanta, ya saṅkan(138) mətu(139) śabda tar yukti(140), tan karasa ikaṅ trikāya(141) paramārtha, ya ta mataṅyan prihən kayatnakna ikaṅ viṣayaniṅ iruṅ, de saṅ sevaka dharma.

Translation

Ghrāṇa means nose. What is the sphere of the nose? It serves as an instrument to smell fragrant scents (surabhi), stinking smells (asurabhi), extraordinary smells (asādhāraṇa). Surabhi means fragrant, asurabhi means stinking and musty, asadhāraṇa means singed smell (aṅit). Therefore three are the kinds of smells. When you smell the fragrance, your heart is happy, when you smell stink and must, your heart is not happy, then your anger appears, that is the cause for the inappropriate speech to arise. The three bodily domains of ultimate truth (trikāya paramārtha) are not experienced. That is the reason why the sphere of the nose must be properly paid attention to and seriously cared for by the servant of the dharma.

2.5

jihvā ṅaraniṅ lidah, apa viṣayaniṅ lidah, pinakapaṅrasanikaṅ(142) ṣaḍrasa(143) ṅaranya, lavaṇa, kaṭuka(144), tikta(145), amla, kaṣāya, madhura, ya sinaṅguh ṣaḍrasa(146), ṅa, lavaṇa hasin, kaṭuka(147) *lada, tikta(148) pahit, amla hasəm, kaṣāya vuduk, madhura manis, nahan(149) sinaṅguh ṣaḍrasa, ṅa, kabhuA:7vkti kunaṅ ika denta jənəka(150) ri sakita(151) denya, hayva ta kita maṅkana, təka kasādhyan(152) deniṅ viṣayanya, ya ta ketu(153) tumiṅgalakna(154) kārya hayu ri saṅ guru, tan karasa ikaṅ trikāya maṇḍala pariśuddha yar(155) maṅkana.

Translation

Jihva means tongue. What is the sphere of the tongue? It serves as an instrument of six flavours, namely: salty (lavaṇa), spicy (kaṭuka), bitter (tikta), sour (amla), savory / astringent (kaṣāya), sweet (madhura). These are called the six flavours (ṣaḍrasa).

2.6

vāk ṅaraniṅ śabda, apa viṣayaniṅ śabda(156), tar tutakna sakojarta, vak pāruṣya, capala(157) asta(158), rika durbala masukər(159), tar paṅinakən(160) ri lokapara, kunaṅ ujaranta(161), śabda manis arum vulat manohara, salvirniṅ maṅdadyakən kaviratyanya, mvaṅ karəṅə̄ deniṅ lokapara, maṅkana ta kita rumakṣa śabda.

Translation

Lavaṇa means salty, kaṭuka is spicy, tikta bitter, amla sour, kaṣāya savory, madhura sweet. Those are called the six flavours (ṣaḍrasa). If they are all enjoyed by you, you will be addicted to becoming sick because of them. You should not do so. Its object [of the tongue] becomes a goal to be realized. That is a sign that you will abandon the good works toward the teacher. If so then the pure circle of three bodily domains are not experienced.

2.7

tvak ṅaraniṅ kuA:8rlit, apa(162) viṣayaniṅ kulit, *ṅarasa *ṅahdap(163) panas tis, ikaṅ maṅkana, kaliṅanya, ya prah(164) pəgəṅən juga ikaṅ viṣaya mijil(165) saka(166) ri kulit.

Translation

Vak means sound. What is the sphere of the sound? All your words should not have to be followed: harsh words, aggressive behaviour, in the situation of powerless and in trouble, it will not make the other human beings feel at ease. However what you should say is the words that are sweet, gentle, and stealing hearts in appearance.
