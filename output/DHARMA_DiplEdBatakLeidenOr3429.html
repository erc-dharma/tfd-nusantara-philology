1r-7 1r-8–1r-8hasa daIda ma karorobo ni karo·¡bo!⟨bano·⟩ta Inano· hasa da(b) /oto monaṁ kita di kasukutano·ni, • This segment is a significant example of the slightly different functions played by the conjunction asa. In the first occurrence is used for introducing a sequel of actions, and more specifically, for introducing the main clause of this sequence. In the second place, it introduces the consequence that the main clause has for the secondary clause. In this last occurrence, asa is identical to the other conjunction umbaen. In the text, it will not be always so clear which of these functions should be attributed to asa, and for this reason I will consider it generally as a conjunction introducing a sequence of actions, which also in TBG 317 is defined as its original function. Consequently, exceptions are made for cases like this one, where it is clear the different meaning that should be attributed to asa and where it has been possible to give two different translations into English, in the extant cases, it will be generally translated as “so”.
